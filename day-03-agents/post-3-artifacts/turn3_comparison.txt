# Textile Manufacturing Incentives vs. Electronics Components Scheme: A Head-to-Head Comparison

*Sources: "Scaling up manufacturing in 7 strategic sectors," "Textile, sports goods, and village industries," and "Indirect Taxes: energy, critical minerals, aviation, electronics"*

---

## 1. Financial Outlay — The Headline Numbers

| Dimension | Electronics Components | Textiles |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary scheme** | Electronics Components Manufacturing Scheme (ECMS) | Integrated Textile Programme (5 sub-schemes) |
| **Announced outlay** | **₹40,000 crore** (revised upward from original ₹22,919 crore) | **Not explicitly stated** in the speech |
| **Scale signal** | Doubled in one budget cycle; investment commitments already at "double the target" | Called "Integrated" — consolidation and new investment, but no single headline figure given |
| **Companion schemes** | ISM 2.0 (semiconductors), Rare Earth Corridors, Hi-Tech Tool Rooms | Mega Textile Parks, Mahatma Gandhi Gram Swaraj initiative |

This is perhaps the starkest contrast: **Electronics gets a hard, very large number — ₹40,000 crore** — while the textile programme, though ambitious in breadth, carries **no disclosed aggregate outlay** in the speech. The electronics allocation is one of the largest single-scheme manufacturing outlays in the entire budget.

---

## 2. Programme Architecture — Breadth and Focus

### Electronics: Targeted, Supply-Chain-Focused
The electronics push is **narrow and deep** — concentrated on filling specific gaps in the value chain:

- **Electronics Components Manufacturing Scheme (₹40,000 crore):** Focused on deepening domestic component production. Launched in April 2025; the Budget capitalises on early over-performance to nearly double the outlay.
- **India Semiconductor Mission 2.0 (ISM 2.0):** Moves beyond chip fabrication to producing **manufacturing equipment and materials**, designing **full-stack Indian IP**, and fortifying supply chains. Industry-led R&D and training centres included.
- **Rare Earth Corridors:** Four states — Odisha, Kerala, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu — will build dedicated corridors for mining, processing, research, and manufacturing of rare earth permanent magnets, which are critical inputs for motors and electronics.
- **Hi-Tech Tool Rooms:** Two CPSE-run, digitally automated facilities to design, test, and manufacture high-precision components locally.

The logic is a **vertically integrated push**: mine rare earths → process them → make components → assemble finished electronics — all domestically.

### Textiles: Wide, Covering the Entire Ecosystem
The textile programme is **broad and horizontal** — five distinct sub-schemes covering different parts of the industry:

| Sub-Scheme | Focus |
|---|---|
| **National Fibre Scheme** | Self-reliance in natural fibres (silk, wool, jute), man-made fibres, and new-age fibres |
| **Textile Expansion and Employment Scheme** | Capital support for machinery, technology upgradation, and common testing/certification centres in traditional clusters |
| **National Handloom and Handicraft Programme** | Integrates existing schemes; targeted support for weavers and artisans |
| **Tex-Eco Initiative** | Globally competitive and sustainable textiles/apparels |
| **Samarth 2.0** | Skilling ecosystem upgrade through industry–academia collaboration |

Plus two standalone programmes:
- **Mega Textile Parks** (challenge mode): Focused especially on value addition in **technical textiles**
- **Mahatma Gandhi Gram Swaraj initiative**: Khadi, handloom, and handicrafts; global market linkage and branding; supports One-District-One-Product and rural youth

## 4. Customs Duty Support

This is where Electronics gets **additional tailored relief** that Textiles largely does not:

### Electronics-Specific Customs Relief *(Section: Indirect Taxes — energy, minerals, aviation, electronics)*
- **Microwave oven components** (tariff item 8516 50 00): BCD reduced to **Nil** — described explicitly as being "to deepen value addition in the consumer electronics sector"
- **Lithium-ion cell capital goods** extended to Battery Energy Storage Systems: BCD to **Nil**
- **Rare earth processing capital goods**: BCD exemption announced

### Textiles-Specific Customs Relief *(Indirect Tax sections)*
- **Hydrophilic and hydrophobic non-woven fabrics** for adult diapers: exemption **lapsing** from 1 April 2026 (i.e., duty relief is being *removed* for this downstream textile input)
- **Re-export window**: 6 months → **12 months** for garment/leather exporters
- **Shoe uppers**: duty-free input imports extended

> **Key insight:** Electronics receives **proactive duty cuts on inputs** to stimulate domestic manufacturing. Textiles receives **trade facilitation** (longer windows, more eligible products) but also sees some exemption rationalisation on the input side.
